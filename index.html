Featured work

Latent Profile Explorer

Open the explorer → · Code · Methods

What it does. Latent profile analysis identifies groups with similar response patterns rather than starting with groups defined in advance. Using synthetic employee-experience data, the project finds four profiles and shows what changes with three or five.

Why it matters. It reveals a group reporting especially negative experiences whose members are spread across departments, tenure groups, and levels. This pattern could be missed in a standard HR dashboard.

About

I have spent the past eleven years at BSCS Science Learning, including eight years in research roles. I began in a clerical position, moved into application support, and eventually became a research scientist. That pathway has shaped how I approach my work. I enjoy the methodological side of research, including survey and instrument design, psychometrics, multilevel modeling, and mixed-methods research, but I am equally interested in making the results accessible and useful. That may mean developing a dashboard, automating an analysis process, or finding a clearer way to communicate the findings.

Alongside my research responsibilities, I co-chair our IRB, contribute the measurement and analysis sections of NSF proposals, and co-lead our AI task force. These roles have given me experience thinking carefully about research ethics, data privacy, participant protection, and the responsible use of new technologies.

Many of the technical skills I use today are ones I taught myself because a project or problem required them. I enjoy working through unfamiliar problems, learning what I need, and building something that makes the work more useful for others. The projects on this site reflect that approach.
